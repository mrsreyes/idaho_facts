Interesting Facts About Idaho

Facts about Idaho

Idaho is the 39th most populated state in the United States. Idaho is located in the NorthWestern Region bordering against six other states. This includes Utah, Washington, Wyoming, Montana, Nevada, and Oregon.

Idaho is known for its mountainous landscapes, and vast swaths of protected wilderness and outdoor recreation areas. The capital, Boise, is set in the Rocky Mountain foothills and is bisected by the Boise River, which is popular for rafting and fishing. The city’s riverfront Julia Davis Park is a downtown green space containing a rose garden, museums and a zoo.

Idaho gets its name from a lobbyist named George M. Willing that offered the name, Idaho. He claimed the word came from the Shoshone Indians and the meaning was "Gem of the Mountains". It's lated found out that he made up the word.

Idaho was admitted to the Union on Thursday, July 3rd, 1890.

Geography

Size: 83,642 square miles

Counties: 44

Famous Locations: Craters of the Moon National Monument, Sun Valley Resort, Lake Coeur d’Alene, Shoshone Falls, Bear River Massacre Historical Site

Fun Facts

  1. Idaho produces one-third of all potatoes grown in the U.S.
  2. Idaho's Boise State University Broncos play on the only blue football field in the world.
  3. More than 72 gemstomes are mined from Idaho.
  4. Idaho has over 3,100 miles of river, which is more than any other state in the nation.
  5. The largest diamond in the United States was found in Idaho.
